## Unit I: Developing Personal and Professional Self
*Success in social work requires a harmonious balance between the individual identity and the professional persona.*

### 1. The Duality of Self
| **The Personal Self** (Off the Clock) | **The Professional Self** (Career Identity) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Identity:** Cultural background, personality, and beliefs. | **Professional Identity:** How you view yourself in your job role. |
| **Personal Values:** Principles that guide your behavior and choices. | **Skills & Competencies:** Technical, analytical, and interpersonal skills. |
| **Emotional Well-being:** Stress management and healthy self-care. | **Career Goals:** Roadmaps for advancement and skill-building. |
| **Relationships:** Bonds with family/friends that provide support. | **Reputation:** Reliability, work ethic, and competence. |
| **Growth:** Hobbies, passions, and personal improvement. | **Work-Life Integration:** Managing healthy professional boundaries. |

### 2. Professional Ethics: The 6 Pillars
1.  **Responsibility:** Owning your duties and considering their impact on the community.
2.  **Accountability:** Being transparent and willing to justify your decisions.
3.  **Loyalty:** Being faithful to the organization/client and maintaining confidentiality.
4.  **Commitment:** Dedication and "going the extra mile" for quality results.
5.  **Cultural Sensitivity:** Respecting diversity and avoiding discrimination.
6.  **Competence:** Continuous learning to stay current with industry trends.

### 3. Productivity & Time Management


[Image of the SMART goals framework]

* **SMART Goals:** Ensure goals are **S**pecific, **M**easurable, **A**chievable, **R**elevant, and **T**ime-Bound.
* **Time Management:** * **Prioritize:** Rank tasks by urgency/importance.
    * **The Pomodoro Technique:** Focus in short bursts with scheduled breaks.
    * **Delegation:** Outsourcing tasks to free up time for critical responsibilities.

## Unit II: Planning for Field Work Practice
*Moving from academic theory to impactful community engagement.*

### 1. The 10-Step Learning Plan
1. **Define Objectives** | 2. **Needs Assessment** | 3. **Develop Plan** | 4. **Design Modules** | 5. **Select Strategies**
2. **Reflective Practice** | 7. **Monitor & Evaluate** | 8. **Community Engagement** | 9. **Ethics** | 10. **Continuous Learning**

### 2. Communication and Rapport
* **Building Rapport:** Use active listening, eye contact, and find common ground.
* **Initiating Dialogue:** Use **open-ended questions**; be patient with silences to allow reflection.
* **Trust:** Consistency, honesty, and involving the community in collaborative decisions.

### 3. Curriculum by Age Group
* **Children:** Literacy, numeracy, hygiene, and creative play.
* **Adolescents:** Decision-making, substance abuse prevention, and digital safety.
* **Youth:** Leadership, financial literacy (budgeting), and resume writing.
* **Elderly:** Health/nutrition, technology use, and "legacy" storytelling.

## Unit III: Documentation in Field Work
*Vital for legal compliance, progress tracking, and professional ethics.*

### 1. Case Records (Individuals)
* **Contents:** Identifying info, family background, assessments, and progress notes.
* **Core Principle:** Must be **Objective**. Record facts and observations, not personal opinions.

### 2. Group Work Records
* **Contents:** Attendance, activity logs, and **Group Dynamics** (relationships/conflicts).
* **Purpose:** To evaluate if group interventions are meeting objectives.

### 3. Community Profile
A data-driven snapshot of the environment:
* **Geography:** Location, climate, and size.
* **Demographics:** Population count, diversity, and age distribution.
* **Socio-economics:** Employment, literacy, and infrastructure.
* **Assets vs. Challenges:** Local resources/leaders vs. crime or health issues.

## Unit IV: Application of Skills and Techniques
*Frameworks for self-awareness and community mobilization.*

### 1. The Johari Window (Self-Awareness)


[Image of the Johari Window model]

* **Open / Arena:** Known to You and Others. (Goal: Expand this through disclosure).
* **Hidden / Fa√ßade:** Known to You, but kept secret from Others.
* **Blind Spot:** Known to Others, but not You. (Fix this through feedback).
* **Unknown:** Talent or potential that neither you nor others have discovered yet.

### 2. SWOT Analysis (Strategic Planning)


[Image of a SWOT analysis matrix]

* **Strengths (Internal):** Your unique skills and advantages.
* **Weaknesses (Internal):** Gaps in knowledge or resources.
* **Opportunities (External):** Favorable trends you can leverage.
* **Threats (External):** External challenges or competitors.

### 3. Mobilizing Support
* **Networking:** Building long-term relationships with influencers.
* **Advocacy:** Using research and platforms to influence policy change.
* **Public Relations (PR):** Storytelling and media engagement to manage reputation.
